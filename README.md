# colorectal-cancer-detection

**Colorectal Cancer (CRC) Detection** refers to the identification of cancer or precancerous states in the colon and rectum at an early stage. Early detection is very important as this cancer develops quite slowly and does not show any signs in its initial stages. The earlier the disease can be detected, the higher chances of successful treatment will be.

In this project, we are going to develop a solution for the early identification of the risk of colorectal cancer based on **gut microbiome analysis** and **lifestyle information**. Gut microbiome includes all the microorganisms living in the human intestine. Recent researches showed the connection between the alterations in microbial composition and the risk of colorectal cancer. Such aspects of people's lifestyles as age, diet, smoking, drinking alcohol, physical activity, body mass index (BMI), and family history are also relevant to the problem.

The system developed within the framework of this project utilizes machine learning algorithms in order to classify patients into two groups based on the biological and lifestyle factors described above. The methods of Explainable AI (XAI) such as SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) are used in order to ensure the interpretability of the predictions.

### Objectives

* Identify the presence of colorectal cancer risk at an early stage.
* Examine gut microbiome and lifestyle factors.
* Build an efficient machine learning-based predictive model.
* Generate interpretable predictions using Explainable AI approach.
* Help healthcare professionals in early prevention and detection of diseases.

### Applications

* Cancer screening programs.
* Decision support systems in clinical practice.
* Personalized healthcare.
* Risk assessment for intervention.
* Scientific research on disease prediction based on microbiome.

### Keywords

**Colorectal Cancer, Machine Learning, Explainable AI (XAI), Gut Microbiome, SHAP, Risk Prediction, Healthcare Analytics, Early Detection, Lifestyle Factors, Disease Classification.**
